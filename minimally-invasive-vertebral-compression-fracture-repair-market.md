<p><p>The Minimally Invasive Vertebral Compression Fracture Repair market is primarily categorized into two types: vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty devices. Vertebroplasty involves the injection of bone cement into fractured vertebrae to stabilize the structure and alleviate pain. Kyphoplasty, on the other hand, includes a balloon-assisted technique that creates space within the vertebra before cement injection, effectively restoring vertebral height and reducing deformity. Both methods aim to treat spinal fractures with minimal tissue damage and expedited recovery.</p></p>
