<p><p>Oil Condition Monitoring (OCM) involves assessing the quality of lubricants and fuels to ensure optimal performance and longevity of machinery. The market is divided into two types: on-site and off-site. On-site monitoring occurs at the equipment location, allowing for immediate analysis and faster decision-making. Off-site monitoring, conversely, involves collecting oil samples for laboratory analysis, providing a more detailed understanding of oil condition and contamination over time. Both methods play crucial roles in predictive maintenance and operational efficiency.</p></p>
