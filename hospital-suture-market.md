<p><p>The hospital suture market is primarily segmented into absorbable and non-absorbable sutures. Absorbable sutures are designed to break down over time in the body, eliminating the need for removal; they are commonly used in internal tissues and wounds that heal quickly. Non-absorbable sutures, on the other hand, remain intact indefinitely and are typically used for external wounds or in situations requiring long-term support. Both types play crucial roles in surgical procedures, influencing healing and patient outcomes.</p></p>
