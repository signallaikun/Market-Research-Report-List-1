<p><ul><li>Discrete</li><li>Integrated</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Graphics Add-in Boards (AIBs) can be classified into two main market types: discrete and integrated. Discrete AIBs are standalone graphics cards that provide dedicated processing power for rendering complex graphics, making them ideal for gaming and professional applications. In contrast, integrated graphics are embedded within the CPU or motherboard, sharing system resources, which makes them suitable for basic tasks like web browsing and office applications. The choice between discrete and integrated depends on performance needs and budget considerations.</p></p>
