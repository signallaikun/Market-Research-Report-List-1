<p><p>The animal antimicrobials and antibiotics market is segmented into various classes based on their chemical structure and mechanism of action. Tetracyclines are broad-spectrum antibiotics effective against a variety of bacteria. Penicillins target specific bacterial cell wall synthesis. Sulfonamides inhibit bacterial growth by interfering with folic acid synthesis. Macrolides and Lincosamides disrupt protein synthesis, while Aminoglycosides target bacterial ribosomes. Fluoroquinolones act on bacterial DNA synthesis, and Cephalosporins offer a range of coverage similar to penicillins, with enhanced effectiveness against resistant strains. Other classes and formulations include peptides and combinations for specific therapeutic needs.</p></p>
